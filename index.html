New Topic Introduced by Anu George Canjanathoppil
Girls go missing daily in India with no effective mechanism to find them. If not found within 24 hours, they are likely sold across borders — trafficked for sex, labor, drug running, or online sexual abuse. Police apathy, bribery, and lack of information sharing compound the problem.
Two Strategic Approaches Debated
Option 1 — Top-Down (Partnership Model)
Collaborate with the public justice system + civil society. Potentially launch with presidential backing to onboard all police stations nationwide. Concern: India may not be institutionally ready. High resistance risk.
Option 2 — Bottom-Up (Crowd-Sourced Aggregator)
Start as a grassroots tool for NGOs struggling with police apathy. Could attract justice system interest over time. Requires access to all police station IDs across India. Philippines national police noted as more receptive; India less so.
Proposed MVP Scope
Team agreed to focus rather than try to solve everything at once. Recommended path: (1) identify all user personas, (2) map how each interacts with the system, (3) pick one niche for MVP — suggested: platform for parents/guardians to post missing child information, (4) public broadcast with location-based feed, (5) easy NGO onboarding.

07 — Selected Project

SafeTrace AI — Missing Children Intelligence & Response Platform

Confirmed direction as of April 7, 2026 · GovTech + NGO model · Proposed by Anu George Canjanathoppil

"A GovTech + NGO AI platform that transforms missing child response from passive reporting to active intelligence — aggregating data across law enforcement, NGOs, and public channels to reduce recovery time through facial recognition, pattern detection, and real-time coordination."
91,296
Children reported missing in India in 2023 — 17.7% rise from 2021 (NCRB, Rajya Sabha Q407, Dec 2025)
8M
Missing children globally / year (ICMEC)
5%→50%
TrackChild improved recovery rate (PwC/DARPG, 2016) — yet manual match rate remains only ~40%
<10
Indian states with functional digital tracking (of 28)
The Problem — Why Existing Systems Fail
Fragmented — police, NGOs, 1098 Childline, TrackChild, and state systems operate in silos with no shared intelligence layer
Reactive — reports collected but patterns rarely detected across districts or state borders
Opaque — families describe the experience as a "black box"; no visibility into actions being taken
Slow — cross-district and cross-state coordination relies on manual communication
First 24–48 hours are the most critical window — yet no coordinated alert system exists
Existing Platforms & Their Gaps
PlatformGap
TrackChild (Govt.)No AI, no cross-agency intelligence, no public interface
Khoya Paya (Govt.)Static database, no pattern detection, no alerts
1098 ChildlineCall-based only; no data integration with police
AMBER Alert (US)US-only; requires law enforcement to initiate; no AI
SafeTrace AIFirst unified AI intelligence layer connecting all stakeholders
Core Platform Modules
Phase 1 MVP — Capstone POC Scope
👁️ Facial Recognition Engine PRIMARY POC ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Matches uploaded photos of missing children against sightings and cross-database records using face embeddings + similarity matching. Age-progression modelling for long-term cases. Reduces identification time from weeks to days/hours.
🧠 NLP-Based Case Intelligence SECONDARY POC ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Entity extraction, topic modelling, and case clustering on FIRs, NGO reports, and tip submissions. Surfaces hidden patterns and linked cases invisible to any single agency.
🔗 Unified Data Aggregation
Ingests reports from police stations, NGOs, 1098 Childline, public tip submissions, TrackChild, and Khoya Paya into one intelligence layer
📱 Verified Public Tip Interface
Citizens can safely report sightings with photo/location — AI-assisted verification filters noise before reaching investigators
📊 Case Tracking Dashboard
Gives families, NGOs, and oversight bodies real-time visibility into case status, assigned officers, and actions taken — ending the black box experience
🌐 Cross-Border Coordination
Structured protocols for cross-state and cross-district case handoffs, alert propagation, and inter-agency communication
Phase 2 Roadmap — Post-Capstone (High Ethical/Legal Complexity)
📍 Geo-Alert / Location-Based System
Proximity-based alerts to citizens and NGO field workers. Requires live location feeds, DPDP Act compliance for minors' data, and significant legal/ethical risk mitigation. Not feasible for capstone scope.
🔮 Pattern Intelligence & Trafficking Corridors
Graph analytics detecting trafficking routes, coordinated disappearances, and repeat perpetrator networks across state boundaries. Requires significant data volume to be statistically reliable.

08 — Research Findings

What We Know So Far

Data points, market context, and domain knowledge gathered ahead of Milestone 1

Problem Scale — India Data
91,296 children reported missing in 2023 — 17.7% rise from 77,535 in 2021 (NCRB; Rajya Sabha Unstarred Q No. 407, Dec 3, 2025)
Girls = 75% of all missing children (68,835 of 91,296 in 2023); girls aged 16–18 = 37,776 — largest at-risk cohort
High-burden states (2022): West Bengal (12,455), MP (11,352), Tamil Nadu (7,009), Delhi (6,150), Bihar (6,000)
Delhi alone: 14,509 women (18+) untraced at end of 2023 — underscoring systemic backlogs
3,000+ active child protection NGOs in India — none share a common data layer
Fewer than 10 of 28 Indian states have functional digital tracking systems
Global missing children: ~8 million annually (ICMEC)
Existing Ecosystem — Key Players
TrackChild (Govt.) — improved recovery from 5% → 50% (PwC/DARPG 2016); 80,000+ transactions/year. Manual match rate only ~40%. Integrated with Mission Vatsalya from Sept 2, 2024. No AI layer, no cross-agency intelligence.
Cri-MAC (2020) — Crime Multi Agency Centre; 24×7 national crime info sharing platform. LEA-facing; no public interface.
NDHTO — National Database of Human Trafficking Offenders. LEA-facing trafficker registry; not integrated with missing child tracking.
Khoya Paya (Govt.) — public missing person listing. Static, no alerts, no pattern detection.
1098 Childline — distress helpline. Call-based only; no data integration with police systems.
ReUnite App (Bachpan Bachao Andolan + Amazon Rekognition) — facial recognition for missing children; limited reach, no intelligence layer.
AMBER Alert (US) — public broadcast alerts. Law enforcement-initiated only; US-only; no AI.
Data Sources Available for POC
NCRB (National Crime Records Bureau) — missing persons annual statistics
TrackChild portal — Government of India case data
Khoya Paya portal — public missing person listings
1098 Childline records (with partnership)
ICMEC international databases
State police FIR databases (via API, willing states)
Citizen-submitted sightings (synthetic for POC)
Business Model Considerations
This is a GovTech + Social Impact play — not a pure commercial SaaS
Grant cycles are slow; government procurement is slow — bridge funding strategy critical
Early NGO subscriptions + CSR grants bridge operations while govt contracts are signed
Section 135 (Companies Act): Large Indian corporates required to spend 2% profit on social causes
UNICEF, USAID, Gates Foundation, DFID all fund child protection technology
International licensing model possible for South/Southeast Asia expansion
